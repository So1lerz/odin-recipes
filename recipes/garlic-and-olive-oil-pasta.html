Garlic and Olive Oil Pasta

Home

Description

One of the great classics for the home cook. Garlic and olive oil have long since been paired together, and this dish is one of many takes on this time tested tradition. It brings together the nutty aroma of toasted garlic, with an indulgent emulsion of olive oil and starch, before being garnished with curly parsley and parmigiano reggiano cheese.

Ingredients

  • Garlic
  • Curly parsley (Italian parsley)
  • Olive oil
  • Finely grated parmesan cheese
  • Fusilli pasta
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Table salt

Steps

  1. Peel a whole clove of garlic and cut the crusted ends from the garlic cloves, before slicing into fine slices
  2. Remove the leaves from the stems of the curly parsley and finely chop in a chiffonade, and reserve
  3. Prepare a colander in a nearby sink, and heat two pans, a large saucepan and a frying pan on high heat. In the saucepan, fill with water and when the frying pan is warm, add a generous layer of olive oil
  4. Add salt to the saucepan until the water is salty. If you have an extraction fan, turn this on as the water comes to a rolling boil
  5. Immediately add the slice garlic into the pan when the olive oil is still warm, and not yet shimmering
  6. Allow the temperature to rise, and when the garlic begins to sizzle lightly, drop the temperature of the frying pan to sustain this reaction
  7. Add the pasta to the pan and reduce the heat to a slow boil. Aim to cook the pasta for 8 minutes until al dente
  8. When the garlic reaches a nutty, toasted almond colour, transfer a few ladles of pasta water from the sauce pan to the frying pan to slow the cooking and to begin to form a sauce. The oil and water will not mix, but move the water around with the implement, being careful not to break up the garlic to form globules of oil and the starch helps to emulsify
  9. If the pasta has cooked, reserve to one side or add to the pan at this stage when the sauce is beginning to form
  10. Cook through until the sauce seems to start clinging to the pasta, and then add parmesan cheese and parsley and continue to cook through for a minute or so to take the edge off the fresh parsley
  11. Season the dish throughout, and finish with black pepper and serve onto a warm plate if possible, with an additional coating of parmesan cheese.
