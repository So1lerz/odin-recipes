Bacon and Tenderstem Pasta

Home

Description

This dish is great for a quick and easy meal in the summer. It combines salty umami from British back bacon with the fresh, crisp taste of tenderstem broccoli, unified in a light bechamel sauce and seasoned heavily with cracked, black pepper.

Ingredients

  • Back bacon rashers
  • Tenderstem broccoli
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Table salt
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • Taglatelle Pasta
  • Milk
  • Plain Flour

Steps

  1. First, while the chopping board is clean, take a chef's knife and cut the tenderstem broccoli pieces in half lengthways and then chop to small batons, trying to keep as consistent a thickness as possible.
  2. Reserve the broccoli to one side, and pat dry the bacon rashers using paper towels, before stacking them back to how they were in the packaging
  3. Cut most of the fat from the bacon edge by cutting around the stack of rashers, then dice into large squares
  4. Prepare a colander in a nearby sink, and heat two pans, a large saucepan and a wok on high heat. In the saucepan, fill with water and when the wok is warm, add a glug of olive oil
  5. Add salt to the saucepan until the water is salty. If you have an extraction fan, turn this on as the water comes to a rolling boil
  6. When the olive oil in the wok begins to shimmer, add diced bacon and sprinkle with black pepper and salt, and sear until a thick fond forms on the bacon and sticks to the bottom of the pan
  7. Add pasta to the saucepan, and reduce heat until pasta cooks at a gentle boil. Time pasta for 8 minutes, or until al dente
  8. Add tenderstem batons to the wok, and allow to sit for a moment until they brighten in colour, before tossing with the bacon
  9. Take a ladle, and add one half ladle of starch water from the pasta pot into the wok to steam the tenderstem as it cooks
  10. When the tenderstem softens, add a large knob or two of butter into the pan and drop the heat to allow the butter to melt but not burn
  11. After the butter has melted, add a healthy amount of flour to the pan, about a tablespoon or so, and stir until the flour mixes with the fat in the pan and sticks to the vegetables like a soft, breaded coating
  12. Cook the flour through for around two minutes on medium heat and then begin adding milk in three batches, a little at a time, until the sauce begins to thicken with heat. Ensure the milk is fresh from the fridge to prevent lumps in the sauce
  13. Garnish generously with pepper as the sauce thickens, and remove the pasta from the heat when it is time to do so
  14. Drain the pasta in the colander, and add to the wok to combine. Cook on medium high heat for a further two minutes to raise the pasta up to temperature, and until the sauce is glossy
  15. Serve onto warmed plates if possible
